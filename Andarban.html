Andharban Forest

Andharban Forest is a dense, scenic forest located in the Sahyadri range in Maharashtra. It is known for its rich biodiversity and lush greenery, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and trekkers. The forest is home to various species of flora and fauna, including several endangered animals. Key Aspects of Andharban Forest: Location & Access: Located in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra, accessible via local roads from nearby towns. Trekking Trails: Offers multiple trekking routes through dense forests, with varying difficulty levels. Wildlife Spotting: Home to various species like leopards, deer, and birds. Best Time to Visit: Winter (October-February) for clear views and comfortable weather. Highlights: Bird watching, nature photography, and exploring hidden waterfalls within the forest. Trek Difficulty: Moderate. It takes approximately 3-4 hours to ascend (about 6.6 km). Key Features: Steel railings, ladders, and chains are installed on difficult, steep patches for safety. Best Time to Visit: Monsoon (June-September) for lush greenery and clouds, and winter (October-February) for clear views. Highlights: Sunrise views, panoramic views of nearby forts (Alang, Madan, Kulang, Ratangad), and the Kalsubai Temple. Logistics: Night treks are popular to avoid heat, with food, water, and local guides available at the base and on the trail.
