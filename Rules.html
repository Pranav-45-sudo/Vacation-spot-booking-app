Rules


1. Pre-Trek Preparation - Research the trail**: Know the difficulty, distance, elevation gain, and weather conditions.
- **Fitness check**: Ensure you’re physically prepared for the trek.
- **Pack smart**: Carry essentials like water, snacks, first-aid kit, flashlight, rain gear, and layered clothing.
2. Safety First - **Never trek alone**: Always go with a group or at least one partner.
- **Inform someone**: Share your itinerary with family or friends.
- **Stay hydrated and nourished**: Drink water regularly and eat energy-rich foods.
- **Know emergency contacts**: Keep local rescue or ranger numbers handy.
3. On the Trail - **Follow marked paths**: Avoid shortcuts or unmarked trails to prevent accidents.
- **Pace yourself**: Trek at a comfortable speed; don’t exhaust yourself early.
- **Respect altitude**: Ascend gradually to avoid altitude sickness.
- **Leave no trace**: Carry back all trash, avoid disturbing wildlife, and respect local customs.
4. Gear & Cloth ing - **Proper footwear**: Wear sturdy trekking shoes with good grip.
- **Weather-ready clothing**: Dress in layers to adapt to changing conditions.
- **Trekking poles**: Useful for balance and reducing strain on knees.
5. Mental & Social Rules - **Stay positive**: Trekking can be tough; motivation keeps you going.
- **Help others**: Support fellow trekkers if they struggle.
- **Respect locals**: Be mindful of communities living near trekking routes.
--- 🚨 Risks to Watch Out For - **Altitude sickness**: Symptoms include headache, nausea, and dizziness.
- **Weather changes**: Sudden rain or snow can make trails dangerous.
- **Wildlife encounters**: Keep a safe distance and avoid feeding animals.
- **Overexertion**: Know your limits; pushing too hard can lead to injuries.
