Sandan Valley

Sandhan Valley, located near Samrad village in Maharashtra's Sahyadri range, is a 200-foot-deep and 1.5 km long water-carved canyon known as the "Valley of Shadows". It offers a challenging, adventure-packed trek, often requiring rappelling, crossing narrow, deep gorges, and camping in the wilderness, making it popular for thrill-seekers between November and February. Key Aspects of Sandhan Valley: Location: Situated near Bhandardara (Akola Taluk, Ahmednagar district), it is accessible by road from Mumbai (185 km), Pune (160 km), and Nashik (90 km). The Trek: A 2-day, 1-night activity involving trekking through 200 ft deep, narrow rocky gorges, descending rappelling patches (up to 30-40 feet), and navigating water pools. Best Time to Visit: November to February is ideal for trekking, avoiding the hazardous monsoon conditions when the area is dangerous and sometimes restricted. Adventure Activities: Key attractions include rappelling, rock climbing, canyoning, and camping under the stars. Highlights: The trek features views of Ajoba Hill and Bann Pinnacle, passing through areas where sunlight rarely reaches the ravine floor. Safety Warning: As of late 2025, the Maharashtra Forest Department has restricted access during certain periods (e.g., monsoon) due to safety concerns; checking for bans is essential. Visitors are advised to hire local guides, use appropriate rappelling equipment, and bring sturdy footwear for the rocky terrain.
