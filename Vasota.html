Vasota

Vasota Fort, also known as Vyaghragad, is an ancient, scenic hill fort situated at 1,171 meters in the heart of the Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary, near Bamnoli in Satara district, Maharashtra. Known for its dense, steep jungle treks and stunning views of the Shivsagar Lake and Sahyadri ranges, it was built by Shilahara Chief Bhoja II in the 12th century and later taken by Shivaji Maharaj in 1655. Key Details and Trekking Information: Location: Satara District, Maharashtra, within the Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary. Altitude: Approximately 1,171 meters (3,842 feet) to 4,370 feet. Trek Type: Dense forest trekking, steep climbs,, and panoramic views of the Sahyadri mountains. Access: The trek begins via a boat ride from Bamnoli (across Koyna backwaters) to the Met Indavali base point. Mandatory Requirements: Requires permission from the Forest Department at Bamnoli. Highlights: Old Shiva and Ganesh temples, Nageshwar Cave, panoramic views of the Shivsagar Lake. Best Time to Visit: October to May (avoiding peak monsoons for better trekking conditions). Trek Logistics: Boat Charges: Approximately ₹3,500 for a boat (can accommodate groups). Forest Fee: Approx. ₹30/person entry, ₹100 for Tiger entry fee, and guide fees around ₹200. Difficulty: Moderate to difficult due to the dense forest terrain. Duration: The trek to the top usually takes about 3-4 hours one way. Important Tips: Start early from Bamnoli to avoid the afternoon sun and complete the trek before dark. Pack enough food and water as there are no shops on the trek route. It is a protected area, so littering is strictly prohibited.
